# Paint Instructions

These instructions are provided to the user when creating a drawing of a Gearbots tank strategy.
They can also be used when interpreting a given drawing.

## Interpretation tips

- In ambiguous scenarios, interpret "favorably", helping the user to create an effective Gearbot strategy

## General instructions

- Use a crude digital Paint tool, or for analog use thick crayons or markers
- Use the style of "Airplane Emergency Instructions" sheets, with multiple "scenarios" to describe strategy and tactics in specific situations
- Scenario's might have 4 walls (full map), some walls (zoomed in on the edge of a map), or no edges (zoomed in on areas towards the center)
- Add very limited text, use only titles for scenarios and labels of things
- Keep drawings simple with lots of basic shapes, icons
- Use arrows and lines liberally

## Legends

Tanks:

- Colored squares are tanks (**red** for your own team, **blue** for enemy bots).
- Draw thick **brown** dotted edges for the wheels (this also indicates what two directions a tank can move in).
- From the center outward a **thick black short** line 

Lines and arrows:

- **Solid thick black** for walls (edges) of the playing arena.
- **Dotted red** for bullets of your own team.
- **Dotted blue** for bullets of the enemy team.
- **Solid thick gray** for primary movement of tanks.
- **Solid thin gray** for secondary or alternative paths of tanks.

Other shapes:

- **Yellow jagged shapes** or **yellow circular set of lines** for bullet hits and explosions
- **Green plus sign** for a healing power up.
- **Green diagonal stripes** for areas to prefer slightly over other areas
- **Pink diagonal stripes** for areas to avoid somewhat
- **Azure blue-green stripes** for areas to focus on when scanning
- **Azure deep dark lines** for preferred scanner (turret heading) locations
